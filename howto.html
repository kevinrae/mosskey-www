How to Use The Moss Key

Observe the moss in hand, matching observations against characters presented in the Key. Continue observing and matching characters while noting that the list of potential matches in Results decreases as the number of characters selected increases. When only one match is presented, check the description page for reference information. The Key presents a suggested ied identification. To be sure, comparison with another identified specimen, review of published information or imagery, or discussion with a bryologist may be necessary.

Some characters are observed with the naked eye while others require magnification. Most suggested identifications possible in this Key do not require use of a microscope with high magnification. Some characters may be best observed with a handlens (10x-20x), while others can best be viewed with a dissecting microscope. Many leaf characters require a cross section of the leaf. Some practice is necessary to obtain a good sample.

At any time, select any genus or species in Results and see reference listings for sources of genus and species descriptions and images in published sources and on the web. If the match in Results indicates a genus or species that you suspect or know is not correct, remove selections one at a time, noting changes in Results until the probable error disappears from Results.

Selecting a genus or species in Results will display characters in the dataset coded to that moss. Taking note of the listed characters, selecting them all in Characters will lead you again to that genus or species in Results. By picking individual characters to delete from Characters may point out at which point in the identification process an error led to an incorrect ID. Or, a suggestion as to which other genera or species can be confused with the moss in question. The Key does not include all characters reported for a genus or a species.

To obtain more detailed information, at the reference link go to Flora North America for more information. On the next screen enter the genus or species in the Search window in the upper right corner. On the next screen select the hyperlinked genus or species name listed to the left. The next screen will display descriptive information and link to a distribution map. Be aware that some mosses do not have descriptions in FNA and some do not have distribution maps.
