Moss Key

A random-access, multiple entry key for the identification of the genera, and selected species, of North American mosses. Based initially on A Perspective Oriented Guide For the Identification of the North American Moss Genera by Malcolm L. Sargent and Diane Lucas, 2006, et seq. Transformated from the dichotomous format by Stephen P. Rae and David Hutton, 2015-2018. Application development by Kevin Rae, 2016-2018.

While the key started as a revision to the Perspective Oriented Guide with its several amendments, the current authors (Stephen Rae and David Hutton) volunteered to revise the Guide by converting the content from dichotomous and trichotomous subkeys to a digital random-access, multiple-entry key on the web. Along the way additional information was obtained from recent scientific publications, professional training curricula, and personal communications. While a reference to Flora North America is displayed for each taxon, a listing of all published and unpublished sources is not presented.

Note that there are entries for a genus such as Grimmia, and also for several species within that genus, such as Grimmia alpicola. In some cases, only the genus is listed since additional information on its species has not yet been added to the dataset. In many genera getting to the genus may not be difficult while going further to the species may require careful dissection and use of a light microscope. After deciding on a species identification, bryologists often compare the specimen to a known, identified specimen in a herbarium. Not everyone has ready access to a herbarium. But, having gotten somewhere in this key means reducing the packets sent off for ID, increasing confidence in identification skills, and saving time when looking for specialists and sending the packet off for identification. Referring to additional information recently published in Flora North America, volumes 27 and 28, provides opportunity to obtain pertinent additional descriptive text and frequent imagery. And, of course, a Google search on the scientific name will lead to other informational sources and imagery.
